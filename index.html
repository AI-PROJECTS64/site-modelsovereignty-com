Model Sovereignty

A neutral, category-defining banner for the emerging concept of model sovereignty: who controls the weights, the inference and the alignment of foundational AI models.

Concept & context

Defining the borders of AI in a fragmented world.

Model sovereignty refers to the strategic capacity of a nation, institution or industrial coalition to design, train, deploy and align its foundational AI models under its own jurisdiction — rather than depending entirely on foreign platforms.

In the 2025–2035 horizon, the question is no longer only “Where is my cloud?” but “Who really controls my models, their behaviour and their failure modes?”

ModelSovereignty.com is an independent digital asset positioned as a potential banner for initiatives in:

  • sovereign LLM and AI strategy,
  • defence, aerospace and critical infrastructure,
  • AI governance, red-teaming and model risk management.
Three strategic pillars

Jurisdictional control

Where do the model weights reside? Under which laws? Who can compel access to them in a crisis or dispute?

Inference security

Who holds the encryption keys and controls inference pipelines, telemetry and resilience under adverse conditions?

Alignment autonomy

Who defines the norms, refusal policies and guardrails that govern what the model will and will not do?
